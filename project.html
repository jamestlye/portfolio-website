1. Eye tracking goggles

The Eye-tTracking Goggles track a patient's glaze and map it onto the real world while measuring data for clinic diagnostic purposes. I worked on both the hardware and the software of the system as well as other complementary projects some are listed below. The goggles are built through 3D printed materials with some off-the-shelf electronics and customized camera components. It is able to track movement at a high frame rate with no visible light.

The goggles use the images of the eyes to calculate both in 2D and 3D where the pupils are and where they are looking. I highly recommend checking out the other videos and images for this one.

4. Ball Catch Device

The Ball Catch Device is a complementary project for the Eye-racking goggles with the goal of incorporating active tests to analyze the hand-eye coordination performance of the patient based on the eyes and the hands.

The device is made with flexible 3D printed TPE "rings" that go on the finger to capture the gesture of the hand, which is interpreted by the Arduino and the output gets sent to the main test computer along with the eyes and other complementary test data.
